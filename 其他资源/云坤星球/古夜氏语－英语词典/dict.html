Convention

“-Z” cannot be used in place of “/HK” as subject when the sentence is not imperative. For example, NCS /HK NK.

BPNT is used in place of “BPL BPL” to mean that “something can / may be possible”, or “maybe someone is capable of something”.

PLN is often used in imperative sentences after “-Z” to show sincerity.

Future hopes after S//N do not need future tense indicator L\T.

In different context, /DN and /DG may either refer to the subject / object of the whole previous sentence, or that of a clause in it.

When /DS is the last word of a sentence, it can be omitted in non-formal context.
